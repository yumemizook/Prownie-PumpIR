FAQ

What is this site?

This is a website for tracking and sharing scores of the game "Pump It Up", dedicated for home users.


But wait, don't we have piugame.com a.k.a the official site already?

Yes, we do. However, piugame.com is a website for arcade users, and is not dedicated for home users (unless you happen to have a cabinet hooked up to the Andamiro network).


How do I upload a score?

You can upload a score by going to the score page and clicking the "Upload Score" button.


What is Pumpbility? How do I get it?

Pumpbility is a measure of how good you are at Pump It Up.

It is calculated based on your grade and the difficulty of the chart you played.

Detailed Pumpbility data can be found at this spreadsheet. We expanded the data for level 29 and beyond.

You can also see your Pumpbility on your profile page.

We are working on changing the calculator for this to more accurately match the Pumpbility to each score.


I play on StepF1/F2/P1/PumpSanity, why does this page uses the Phoenix scoring system?

This is intentional. Combo-based scoring is an outdated mess and the score can't really be calculated without detailed input data, thus requiring more user input.

We have instead opted in for a capped scoring system at 1,000,000 points (1:1 Phoenix scoring system). This is to make it easier for users to understand and compare scores.

Just input the judgement data, and the site will automatically calculate the score for you.


I'm seeing ridiculous scores on the leaderboards. Are these real?

Yes and no.

Due to the nature of the current score submission system, it is possible to submit scores that are not legitimate.

We are working on a more accurate score submission system, but until then, please let us know by submitting a report on the score page.

As always, if you are caught submitting scores that are not legitimate, your account will be banned for a really, really long time. Don't do it!

P.S. If you see someone getting a Perfect Game on 1948 D32 they are 100% cheating

I'm seeing a score that is not legitimate!

If you see a score that is not legitimate, please report it by going to the score page > Report Score.

If we find that the score is not legitimate, we will remove it from the leaderboards and your scores will be removed.

Please contact an admin to let us know and correct the score.

I want to delete my account

If you want to delete your account, go to your profile > Settings > Delete Account. We will delete your account and all your data.

You will not be able to register a new account again after deleting your account for a really, really long time, so think twice before deleting your account!

If you get caught creating an account after deleting your account, it counts as multi-accounting and will be handled as such.


What happens if I get caught breaking the rules?

Depending on the severity of the offense, there are corresponding punishments.

For minor offenses, you will be given a warning.

For larger-scale offenses, you will be excluded from the leaderboards and your scores will be removed.

For severe offenses, you will be banned from the site.


What happens when you get banned?

You will be locked out of the features of this website, such as score submission, profile editing, and more. Basically, the website becomes read-only.

You can know that you got banned by trying to access the website, and seeing a message in red saying "You have been banned from the site."

If you or someone tries to access the profile page, they will see that the user is banned.

If you want to appeal your ban, please contact an admin to let us know. We will look through the case and see if we can lift the ban.

During the meantime, DO NOT create or use other accounts to bypass the ban. We will ban each and every account you create and use, even if you didn't create it yourself.

And no, don't pull the "This is good incentive to get players to get better" card. It's not. I have heard of this and other justifications before, and it's straight up bullshit.

As of right now, there is little means to tell if a user is multi-accounting. We are working on a more accurate system, but until then, one person per account, one account per person. No exceptions.

Again, if you are caught creating an account after deleting your account, it counts as multi-accounting and will be handled as such.
